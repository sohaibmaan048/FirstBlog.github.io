Midterm week started just after one week of classes ended. I was already feeling stressed because online classes had not given me a clear understanding of many concepts. Most of the time, it felt like things were rushed, and I could not fully understand the ideas being taught.

When exams began, the pressure increased even more. Everything felt harder than expected, especially because I had not built a strong foundation during the online lectures.

---

## The toughest subject: OOP 🧠

Object Oriented Programming was the most difficult subject for me. The teaching style focused more on memorizing lines instead of understanding concepts deeply. It felt like we were learning “what to write” instead of “why we write it.”

Because of that, applying concepts in the exam became very difficult.

> **Key Takeaway:** Theoretical memorization is no match for conceptual clarity when it comes to coding exams.

---

## DLD Surprise Challenge 🔌

Digital Logic Design was actually my favorite subject. I understood the basics and enjoyed studying it. But the exam was more difficult than expected. Even though I had interest in it, I did not perform as well as I thought I would.

Still, compared to OOP, I did better in DBMS.

---

## DBMS Performance 📊

Database Management Systems was not very difficult in terms of questions. It was more conceptual. If you understand the logic and structure, the paper becomes manageable.

I performed better in DBMS than in both DLD and OOP because the concepts were clearer to me.

---

## Internship Started During Exams 💼

During my exam period, I also received an opportunity to join an internship at **DevelopersHub Corporation** as a remote intern.

![Remote Internship Journey](/assets/in_post/internship_start.jpg)

This made things more challenging because I had to balance exams and internship communication at the same time. I requested my internship instructor to extend my task submission deadline due to midterm pressure, and they agreed.

---

## After Midterms: The Shift 🔄

After exams ended, I returned home and started focusing fully on my internship tasks. Now I am actively working on completing assignments and improving my skills in a real-world environment.

---

## Final Thoughts 🚀

This midterm period was stressful, but it also became a turning point. I learned how difficult subjects can feel when concepts are not clear, and how important it is to stay consistent even when things overlap.

* ✅ **Resilience:** Handling two major life events at once.
* ✅ **Adaptability:** Transitioning from theory to remote professional work.
* ✅ **Growth:** Learning to communicate needs (like deadline extensions) effectively.

Balancing exams and a new internship was not easy, but it gave me real experience of handling pressure and responsibility at the same time. I’m excited to see where this journey at DevelopersHub leads! 💪
