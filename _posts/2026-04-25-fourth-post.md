## OOP Concepts: Inheritance & Polymorphism 🧠

In Object-Oriented Programming (OOP), my focus shifted to two core pillars: **Inheritance** and **Polymorphism**. These are crucial concepts for writing reusable and flexible code.

- **Inheritance** helped me understand how a subclass can acquire properties and behaviors from a superclass, reducing redundancy.
- **Polymorphism** showed me how the same interface or function can behave differently depending on the context.

While these concepts required some extra mental effort to grasp initially, everything fell into place once the logic clicked.

> **Key Takeaway**
> In programming, mastering the underlying logic is always more valuable than just memorizing syntax.
